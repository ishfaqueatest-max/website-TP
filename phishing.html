🎣 Phishing Attacks

Learn how cyber criminals trick people into giving away personal information.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where criminals pretend to be trusted organisations such as banks, universities, or delivery companies in order to trick people into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.

These attacks often come in the form of emails, text messages, or fake websites that look legitimate but are designed to steal personal data.

Why Students Are Targeted

Students are common targets for phishing because they rely heavily on online services such as university email, banking apps, and cloud storage.

Attackers know students receive many emails from different services, making it easier for fake emails to blend in with legitimate messages.

⚠ Example:
Students sometimes receive fake emails claiming their university account will be suspended unless they verify their login details.

Example of a Phishing Email

Below is an example of a typical phishing email. Attackers often create a sense of urgency to pressure users into acting quickly.

Subject: Urgent – Verify Your University Account

Dear Student,

We have detected unusual activity on your university email account. Please verify your login details immediately using the link below to prevent your account from being suspended.

Verify your account here:
http://university-login-security-update.com

How to Spot Phishing Attempts

  • Suspicious sender email addresses
  • Urgent messages asking for immediate action
  • Links that lead to unfamiliar websites
  • Poor spelling or grammar in emails
  • Requests for passwords or personal information

How Students Can Protect Themselves

  • Always verify emails before clicking links
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Never share passwords via email
  • Use official university websites to log in
  • Report suspicious emails to university IT services
